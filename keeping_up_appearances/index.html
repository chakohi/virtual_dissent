Keeping up appearances
Mendi + Keith Obadike | 2001 | web page
by mendi@blacknetart.com

Mendi Lewis Obadike

Broken up sentences in black text on a white background describe a relationship between a young woman and a mentor. As the mouse hovers over the areas between the black text, lavender text fills in the gaps. This text reveals the otherwise-obscured experience of the narrator , the subtext of interaction, of her feeling pressured by the sexually predatory mentor. It conveys the often-silenced voices of women, showing what is obscured by the gendered norms of interaction and the public narratives of female roles. The user cannot interact to change the narrative, signifying its current inevitability.

Aside from net art, Mendi+Keith, a married Igno Nigerian American artistic duo, have primarily worked on sound and intermedia art, interrogating American culture, especially its racial foundations and expressions, and exploring issues of identity through performance, installation, and recording. Their ongoing series include American Suites (2006), Praise Songs & Installations (2000), Numbers Series (2015), Opera Masquerades (2015), Spirituals (2016), and NavWrks (2017). Each has also authored numerous publications on their work. Kieth Obadike currently works as a professor in the College of Arts and Communications at William Paterson University and as digital media editor at Obsidian. Mendi Obadike works as associate professor in the Department of Humanities and Media Studies at Pratt Institute. Both are art advisors to the times Square Alliance and The Vera List Center for Art and Politics.
